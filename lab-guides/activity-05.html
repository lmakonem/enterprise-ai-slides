Part 1: Policy Critique (15 min)

Read these two short policy excerpts. Identify ONE major flaw in each.

Policy A (The "Wild West"):

"Employees are encouraged to use any AI tool that helps them be more productive. Feel free to use ChatGPT, Midjourney, or whatever you find useful. Just don't share passwords."

  • Critique: What happens to customer data? What if the tool is malware?

Policy B (The "Bunker"):

"The use of any AI, machine learning, or automated decision-making tool is strictly prohibited on company devices. All work must be performed manually by employees. Violation is grounds for termination."

  • Critique: Is this realistic? Will employees just use their personal phones (Shadow AI)? Does it kill innovation?

Part 2: Understanding "Acme Corp" (5 min)

You are writing a policy for Acme Corp, a mid-sized healthcare software company.
* Industry: Healthcare (HIPAA regulated).
* Data: Patient records (PHI), proprietary code, internal financial data.
* Employees: 500 (Developers, Sales, HR, Support).
* Goal: Wants to use AI to speed up coding and customer support, but terrified of data leaks.

Part 3: Drafting the Policy (30 min)

Open the AI Policy Template.
Customize it for Acme Corp.

  1. Define Approved Tools:

    • Hint: GitHub Copilot for Business (for code), Microsoft Copilot (for internal docs).
    • Hint: ChatGPT Enterprise (for marketing, with "no training" toggle).

  2. Define Prohibited Uses:

    • Crucial: "Never upload Patient Health Information (PHI) to ANY public AI tool."
    • Crucial: "Do not use AI to make final hiring decisions without human review."

  3. Define Data Handling:

    • Create a simple classification:
      • Public Data: OK for ChatGPT Free.
      • Internal Data: OK for Copilot Enterprise.
      • PHI/Sensitive: RESTRICTED.

Part 4: Peer Review Checklist (10 min)

Review your draft against this checklist:
1. [ ] Does it explicitly mention "Patient Data" or "PHI"?
2. [ ] Does it list specific approved tools?
3. [ ] Does it require human review of outputs?
4. [ ] Is there a clear reporting channel for incidents?
