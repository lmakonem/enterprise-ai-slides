Part 1: The Basics (15 min)

Open your preferred AI tool. Try these exercises.

  1. Basic (Zero-Shot):

    • Prompt: "Summarize the history of the internet."
    • Goal: Too broad. Refine it to be specific.
    • Refinement: "Summarize the history of the internet in 3 paragraphs, focusing on the invention of TCP/IP and the World Wide Web."

  2. Role-Based (Persona):

    • Prompt: "You are a grumpy 19th-century lighthouse keeper. Explain how a smartphone works."
    • Goal: Notice the tone shift.

  3. Few-Shot (Examples):

    • Prompt:
      > "Convert these colloquial phrases into professional corporate speak.
      >
      > Input: 'That idea is stupid.' -> Output: 'We may want to reconsider that approach.'
      > Input: 'I'm too busy.' -> Output: 'I currently have limited bandwidth.'
      > Input: 'Stop wasting time.' -> Output: "

    • Goal: Teach the model by example.

Part 2: Advanced Techniques (30 min)

Now, let's get serious.

  1. Chain-of-Thought (Reasoning):

    • Prompt:
      > "Solve this logic puzzle. Think step-by-step. Show your work before giving the final answer.
      >
      > Puzzle: A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost?"
    • Goal: Without "think step-by-step," models often guess $0.10 (wrong). The correct answer is $0.05.

  2. Structured Output (JSON/CSV):

    • Prompt:
      > "Generate 5 fake customer personas for a coffee shop app. Return the result strictly as a JSON array with keys: 'name', 'age', 'favorite_drink', 'spending_habit'."
    • Goal: Get clean data you can use in code.

  3. Constraint (Format):

    • Prompt:
      > "Explain quantum computing. Use only words with one syllable. Keep it under 50 words."
    • Goal: Force simplicity.

  4. Multi-Step (Chaining):

    • Prompt A: "Write an outline for a presentation on AI safety."
    • Prompt B: "Take Section 2 of that outline and write the full script for a 3-minute speech."

  5. Meta-Prompting:

    • Prompt: "Write a prompt that I can give to an AI to make it act like a world-class Python tutor."
    • Goal: Use AI to write better AI prompts.
